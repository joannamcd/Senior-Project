Watercolor

Article by: Joanna McDonald ✓

Date Posted: 11/20/2022




Watercolor is a water-based medium applied by brush. The paint itself can come in a solid form, called "pans," or in tubes of thickened paint. When water is added to either form, the paint is activated. Because the water thins the paint, watercolor can be applied layer by layer to gradually add details and dimension to the image. The paper that is usually best suited for watercolor is a white, textured paper. The texture helps to absorb the light layers of paint, and the white paper helps the colors shine through.
